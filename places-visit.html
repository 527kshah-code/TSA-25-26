Places to visit in Monmouth County

Monmouth County is a great place to visit with many attractions and activities to enjoy. Here are some of the top places to visit in Monmouth County:

National Parks

Allaire State Park

Allaire State Park is probably best known for its historic 19th-century iron making town.The Manasquan River’s floodplain provides habitat for over 200 species of wildflowers, trees and plants as well as habitat for birds and other wildlife.

Monmouth Battlefield State Park

Monmouth Battlefield State Park is a historic site that preserves the location of the Battle of Monmouth, a significant engagement during the American Revolutionary War.

Double Trouble State Park

Double Trouble State Park is a beautiful park that offers a variety of recreational activities for visitors.

Shark River Park

Shark River Park is a scenic park that offers opportunities for hiking, picnicking, and wildlife observation.

Cheesequake State Park

Cheesequake is the only park where the northern hardwood forest meets a southern pine barrens ecosystem at sea level offering birders and hikers a remarkable diversity of native flora and fauna in a healthy, natural environment.

Hartshorne State Park

Hartshorne State Park is a scenic park that offers opportunities for hiking, picnicking, and wildlife observation.

Turkey Swamp State Park

Within the northernmost fringes of the Pine Barrens, the Turkey Swamp area is located on a ridge, being a major drainage divide within Central New Jersey

Perrine Hill Trail Head

In addition to access to the lake, there are several trails including the 1.4-mile Lakeside Loop that follows an old farm road

Hartshorne Woods Park

The park has 16 miles of trails, several old military bunkers and a small beach with a fishing pier

Big Brooks Park

Its rolling hills and open meadows make the park a popular destination for hikers, cyclists and wildlife enthusiasts. The park offers ideal access to the southern extension of the Henry Hudson Trail.

Gateway National Recreation Area

It provides recreational opportunities that are not commonly found in a dense urban environment,

Cattus Island County Park

Cattus Island is a park of the Ocean County Department of Parks and Recreation

Ocean County Park

Once the estate of John D. Rockefeller, this large park offers lake activities, picnicking & trails.

Island Beach State Park

Contains outstanding examples of plant communities such as primary dunes, thicket, freshwater wetlands, maritime forest and tidal marshes

Huber Woods Park

take a moment amid the scenic beauty and panoramic views to observe small things - sounds, smells, light and shadow, subtle hints of seasonal change.

Monmouth County Park System -- HQ Offices

Established in 1960, the Monmouth County Park System is an agency that maintains over 40 parks and recreational areas.
